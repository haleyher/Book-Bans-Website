BANNED BOOKS

and why we should bring them back into libraries

What's the Issue?

Banned and challenged books are not something new. The censorship of books have been around for a long time. In 1639, the first book ever banned because it went against the public's beliefs. This is a trend with Censorships of books, as those in authority want to shape our ideology.

Many schools across America are banning books for being too graphic, sexual, possessing crude language, LGBTQ+ content and more. Those in authority, or in some cases the students' parents, want to shape their ideology. I personally believe that these students should be given the chance to read the books and then make the decisions for themselves. I understand in some cases, though. It would be not the best to have a very explicit book in an elementary school. However, the people that need the representation in media or even ones who want to learn the stories from marginalized groups are suffering.

The Importance of the Stories

In some cases, It's not even just marginalized groups. It's the stories that should be spread, but are still being censored. Last year, I read Maus, which is a comic book depicting a story from the Holocaust but with a twist. It represents all of the characters as animals, with Jewish people becoming mice and then the Nazis becoming cats. The book is about a very serious subject matter and treats it well. I personally believe it was a good story to learn about in school to further explore that time period.

Currently, more than 1,000 books have been banned in schools. It's starting to turn into what books like Fahrenheit 451 and 1984 wanted to warn us against.
